# Proven Prompt Patterns

> Reference for /image-gen. Load when crafting prompts to select the right structural pattern for the use case.

## Pattern 1: Photorealistic Scene

For product shots, lifestyle imagery, and realistic environments.

```
A photorealistic [shot type] of [subject], [action/expression], set in [environment].
Illuminated by [lighting], creating a [mood] atmosphere. Captured with a [camera/lens],
emphasizing [textures/details]. [Aspect ratio].
```

**When to use:** Product marketing, hero images, lifestyle content.
**Key lever:** Camera/lens specificity. "85mm at f/2.0" vs "a camera" is the difference between a portrait and a snapshot.

## Pattern 2: Stylized Illustration

For brand graphics, icons, and non-photographic assets.

```
A [style] illustration of [subject], featuring [characteristics] in a [color palette].
The design has [line style] and [shading]. [Background specification].
```

**When to use:** Blog headers, social graphics, brand assets.
**Key lever:** Naming the art style explicitly (isometric, flat, hand-drawn, watercolor).

## Pattern 3: Text in Image

For quote cards, announcement graphics, and branded text visuals.

```
Create a [image type] with the text "[exact text]" in a [font style].
The design is [style], with [color scheme]. Text positioned [placement].
```

**When to use:** Social announcements, quote cards, feature callouts.
**Key lever:** Nano Banana Pro excels at text rendering. Be explicit about font style, size, and placement.

## Pattern 4: Product/Commercial

For e-commerce, app screenshots, and polished commercial imagery.

```
A studio-lit product shot of [product] on [surface]. [Lighting setup] to highlight [feature].
Camera angle [angle] to showcase [detail]. Sharp focus on [element]. [Aspect ratio].
```

**When to use:** Landing pages, App Store assets, ad creative.
**Key lever:** Material descriptions. "Frosted glass with fine matte texture" vs "glass."

## Pattern 5: Minimalist/Negative Space

For blog headers, hero sections, and images that need text overlay space.

```
A minimalist composition featuring [subject] positioned in the [area] of the frame.
Background is [color/texture], creating generous negative space for [purpose].
Soft lighting. [Aspect ratio].
```

**When to use:** Blog headers (leave room for title), hero sections, presentation slides.
**Key lever:** Explicitly say WHERE the negative space should be and WHY.

## Pattern 6: Abstract/Conceptual

For thought leadership, brand mood, and emotional visuals.

```
[Visual metaphor description] representing [concept]. The mood is [feeling].
Colors are [palette]. The composition [arrangement]. Lighting creates [effect]. [Aspect ratio].
```

**When to use:** Abstract concepts (innovation, growth, transformation), mood pieces.
**Key lever:** The visual metaphor. See visual-metaphors.md for the mapping table.

## Pattern 7: Hyper-Detailed Portrait

For personal brands, team photos, and character-driven visuals.

```
age: [range]
vibe: "[a specific scenario that captures the person's energy]"
pose: [exact body position]
expression: [micro-expression details]
clothing: [specific items with textures]
```

**When to use:** Founder photos, team pages, personal brand imagery.
**Key lever:** The "vibe" line. "Cape Town art dealer at 4 AM after closing a million-dollar sale" gives the model more to work with than "confident professional."

## Pattern 8: Era-Specific

For nostalgia, retro aesthetics, and deliberate imperfection.

```
A low-resolution [era] photo taken on a [specific camera/device].
[Era-specific artifacts: flash, grain, color cast, date stamp].
[Subject and scene]. [Specific imperfections that sell the era].
```

**When to use:** Throwback content, retro brand aesthetics, authenticity.
**Key lever:** Name the EXACT camera and year. The artifacts follow from that specificity.

## Craft Tips

- **Describe, don't list.** Flowing narrative > comma-separated keywords.
- **Be tactile.** Name textures: "paper-like", "matte", "wet sheen", "weathered."
- **Degrees matter.** "Chin tilted 3 degrees upward" > "chin up."
- **Light as character.** "Split lighting: half Caravaggio warmth, half cold morning truth."
- **Vibe > adjectives.** Bad: "confident woman." Good: "art dealer at 4 AM, still high on adrenaline and red wine."
- **Imperfections sell realism.** "Faint stain," "one lock falling across cheekbone," "date stamp in corner."
