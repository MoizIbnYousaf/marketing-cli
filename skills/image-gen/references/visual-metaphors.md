## Usage Notes

- **Pick ONE metaphor per image.** Multiple metaphors in one composition creates visual noise.
- **The metaphor should be recognizable but not cliched.** "Lightbulb for idea" is tired. "Fog clearing to reveal a path" is the same concept, freshly rendered.
- **Adapt to brand style.** A "warm tech minimalism" brand renders "creation" as particles assembling in warm light. A "bold and energetic" brand renders it as an explosion of color solidifying into form.
- **When the concept isn't in the table,** ask: "What does this concept LOOK like if you freeze it mid-action?" The answer is usually the metaphor.

## Anti-Metaphors (Kill on Sight)

These metaphors appear in every AI-generated marketing image. Avoid them:

- Floating 3D cubes or blocks (for any concept)
- Blue/purple grid backgrounds (for "tech")
- Human silhouette with glowing brain (for "AI")
- Gears meshing (for "integration" or "process")
- Puzzle pieces connecting (for "teamwork" or "solutions")
- Rocket ship (for "launch" — unless the product literally launches rockets)
- Handshake (for "partnership" — unless the visual is genuinely distinctive)
- Light at the end of a tunnel (for "solutions")
- Iceberg (for "hidden complexity")

If you catch yourself reaching for one of these, stop and find a fresh metaphor from the table above.
