# Launch Checklist

Comprehensive pre-flight checklist organized by category. Work through each section before, during, and after launch. Items are ordered by priority within each section.

---

## Positioning & Messaging

- [ ] One-sentence value proposition written and tested with 3+ people
- [ ] Launch angle defined — what makes this newsworthy NOW (not just "we built a thing")
- [ ] Target audience clearly defined — who this is for and who it is NOT for
- [ ] Key differentiator articulated — why this over alternatives
- [ ] Pricing decided and justified (if applicable)
- [ ] Objection responses prepared for top 3-5 likely pushbacks
- [ ] Tagline/headline A/B variants ready for testing
- [ ] "Elevator pitch" version ready: 15 seconds, 60 seconds, 5 minutes

---

## Landing Page & Website

- [ ] Landing page live with clear headline, subheadline, and CTA
- [ ] Above-the-fold hook communicates value in under 5 seconds
- [ ] Social proof present: testimonials, logos, user counts, or results
- [ ] Email capture or signup form working end-to-end
- [ ] Mobile responsive — tested on actual devices, not just browser resize
- [ ] Page load under 3 seconds (test with PageSpeed Insights)
- [ ] Open Graph and Twitter Card meta tags set for link sharing
- [ ] Favicon and brand assets in place
- [ ] 404 page customized (people will mistype URLs)
- [ ] Legal pages present if needed: terms, privacy, cookie policy

---

## Content & Creative Assets

### Written Content
- [ ] Launch blog post written: problem → solution → proof → CTA
- [ ] Press release or media kit prepared (if doing PR outreach)
- [ ] FAQ document covering top 10 anticipated questions
- [ ] Comparison page drafted (vs top 1-2 competitors)
- [ ] Product documentation or help center seeded with basics

### Visual Assets
- [ ] Product screenshots (clean, annotated, showing key features)
- [ ] Demo video or product walkthrough (60-90 seconds)
- [ ] Social media graphics: announcement, feature highlights, quote cards
- [ ] Feature GIFs for social sharing and Product Hunt
- [ ] Logo files in multiple formats (PNG, SVG, dark/light variants)

### Social Content
- [ ] Launch day posts drafted for each active platform
- [ ] Thread/carousel version for long-form social (Twitter thread, LinkedIn carousel)
- [ ] Community posts drafted (Reddit, Indie Hackers, relevant Slack/Discord groups)
- [ ] Reply templates for common questions and congratulatory messages

---

## Email

- [ ] Email list segmented: power users, new subscribers, VIPs, lapsed
- [ ] Pre-launch tease email drafted (send 3-7 days before)
- [ ] Launch announcement email drafted with clear CTA
- [ ] Follow-up email for non-openers (different subject line)
- [ ] Onboarding email sequence ready (3-5 emails over first 2 weeks)
- [ ] Transactional emails tested: welcome, confirmation, receipt
- [ ] Unsubscribe link present and working
- [ ] Email preview tested across clients (Gmail, Apple Mail, Outlook)
- [ ] Sender domain authenticated (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

---

## Distribution Channels

### Owned Channels
- [ ] Blog post scheduled for launch day
- [ ] Email blast scheduled for launch morning
- [ ] In-app announcement prepared (banner, modal, or tooltip)
- [ ] Website banner or notification bar ready to activate
- [ ] Changelog or release notes updated
- [ ] Community/forum post drafted (if you have a branded community)

### Rented Channels
- [ ] Social profiles updated with launch messaging
- [ ] Posts scheduled across platforms with appropriate timing
- [ ] Product Hunt listing complete: tagline, description, media, first comment
- [ ] Directory submissions prepared (BetaList, AlternativeTo, etc.)
- [ ] App store listing updated (if applicable)

### Borrowed Channels
- [ ] Influencer/creator outreach sent with clear ask and easy-share assets
- [ ] Guest post or newsletter feature confirmed with publication date
- [ ] Podcast appearance scheduled (if applicable)
- [ ] Cross-promotion partners briefed with share templates
- [ ] Affiliate/referral incentives communicated to partners

---

## Analytics & Tracking

- [ ] Analytics installed and verified (page views, events firing correctly)
- [ ] UTM parameter convention defined and documented
- [ ] Conversion goals configured: signup, activation, purchase
- [ ] Funnel tracking set up: visit → signup → activate → convert
- [ ] Referral source tracking working ("How did you hear about us?")
- [ ] Retargeting pixel installed (for post-launch ad campaigns)
- [ ] Real-time dashboard ready for launch day monitoring
- [ ] Baseline metrics recorded (pre-launch numbers for comparison)
- [ ] Error monitoring active (catch crashes before users report them)

---

## Technical Readiness

- [ ] Production deployment tested — full user journey works end-to-end
- [ ] Load testing done if expecting significant traffic
- [ ] CDN configured for static assets and media
- [ ] SSL certificate valid and not expiring soon
- [ ] Database backups running and verified
- [ ] Error alerting configured (PagerDuty, Sentry, etc.)
- [ ] Rollback plan documented in case of critical issues
- [ ] Rate limiting in place for signup/API endpoints
- [ ] Staging environment matches production

---

## Support & Operations

- [ ] Support team briefed on new product/feature and common questions
- [ ] FAQ or help docs published and linked from product
- [ ] Support channels ready: email, chat, or ticket system
- [ ] Escalation path defined for critical issues on launch day
- [ ] Response time expectations set (aim for <1 hour on launch day)
- [ ] Feedback collection mechanism in place (survey, feedback widget, email)

---

## Team Coordination

- [ ] Launch day schedule shared — who does what, when
- [ ] Social media monitoring assignments clear
- [ ] Real-time communication channel set up (Slack channel, group chat)
- [ ] Decision-maker available all day for urgent calls
- [ ] Post-launch debrief scheduled (within 48 hours)
- [ ] Success metrics defined — what does a "successful" launch look like?

---

## Launch Day Protocol

### Morning (Launch Hour)
1. Verify landing page, signup flow, and checkout working
2. Publish blog post
3. Send announcement email
4. Post on all social channels
5. Submit to Product Hunt / directories
6. Activate in-app announcements
7. Notify partners to share

### Throughout the Day
8. Monitor social mentions and respond to every comment
9. Watch analytics dashboard for conversion and error rates
10. Engage in community discussions
11. Share early traction updates with team and supporters
12. Address any technical issues immediately

### End of Day
13. Send thank-you messages to top engagers
14. Compile day-1 metrics snapshot
15. Identify top-performing channels
16. Queue follow-up content for day 2

---

## Post-Launch (First 72 Hours)

- [ ] Day-1 metrics reviewed and shared with team
- [ ] Follow-up email sent to non-openers
- [ ] User feedback collected and categorized
- [ ] Top-performing channels identified for doubling down
- [ ] Critical bugs or UX issues from feedback addressed
- [ ] "Launch results" content published (transparency builds trust)
- [ ] Thank-you messages sent to partners and supporters
- [ ] Retargeting campaigns activated for landing page visitors
- [ ] Onboarding sequence verified — new users receiving emails

---

## Post-Launch (Week 1-2)

- [ ] Post-signup survey analyzed: acquisition channels, objections, satisfaction
- [ ] Case study drafted from best early adopter story
- [ ] Comparison and alternative pages published
- [ ] Content repurposing started: blog → threads → short video → newsletter
- [ ] Referral or sharing mechanism added to product
- [ ] Channel-level CAC calculated — where to invest next
- [ ] Next launch moment planned (feature update, milestone, re-launch)

---

## If Launch Underperforms

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| High traffic, low signups | Weak landing page or unclear value prop | A/B test headline, add social proof, simplify form |
| Low traffic overall | Distribution channels not activated | Push harder on borrowed channels, try paid ads |
| Signups but no activation | Onboarding friction or unclear first step | Simplify first-run experience, add welcome email |
| Good activation, quick churn | Product gap or wrong audience | Survey churned users, revisit positioning |
| No social engagement | Wrong platform or weak content | Test different formats, post at different times |
