# Phased Launch Timeline

Week-by-week breakdown for product launches. Adapt timelines based on product complexity and team size. Solo founders can compress; funded teams should expand for coordination.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (8-6 Weeks Before Launch)

### Week 8: Research & Positioning
- Run audience research — identify where your buyers hang out online
- Analyze 3-5 competitor launches (what channels, what messaging, what worked)
- Define your launch angle — what makes THIS launch newsworthy?
- Draft initial positioning statement (1 sentence: who it's for, what it does, why now)

### Week 7: Asset Planning
- Map your ORB channels — which owned, rented, borrowed channels will you activate?
- Identify 10-20 borrowed channel targets (podcasts, newsletters, influencers)
- Outline content calendar: what gets published when, on which channel
- Set up analytics — UTM naming convention, conversion goals, dashboard

### Week 6: Landing Page & Capture
- Build landing page with clear value prop, social proof placeholder, email capture
- Set up waitlist or early access signup flow
- Install tracking pixels (analytics, retargeting, heatmaps)
- Create lead magnet if applicable (free resource that attracts your target buyer)

---

## Phase 2: Build-Up (5-3 Weeks Before Launch)

### Week 5: Content Creation
- Write launch blog post (problem → solution → proof → CTA)
- Create demo video or product walkthrough (60-90 seconds max for social)
- Design social media assets: announcement graphics, feature GIFs, quote cards
- Draft email announcement sequence (tease → announce → remind → last chance)

### Week 4: Social Proof & Outreach
- Send product to 5-10 beta testers — collect testimonials and case studies
- Screenshot or record early user wins for social proof
- Begin outreach to borrowed channels: pitch guest posts, podcast appearances, newsletter features
- Prepare Product Hunt listing if applicable (tagline, description, media, maker comment)

### Week 3: Distribution Setup
- Schedule social media posts across launch week (use scheduling tools)
- Segment email list: power users, new subscribers, lapsed, VIPs
- Draft all email sequences: pre-launch tease, launch day, follow-up, onboarding
- Confirm all partnership/collaboration dates and deliverables
- Prep in-app announcement: banner, tooltip, or modal for existing users

---

## Phase 3: Pre-Launch Heat (2-1 Weeks Before Launch)

### Week 2: Build Anticipation
- Start teaser campaign on social: "Something's coming" posts with countdowns
- Send first pre-launch email to list (tease the problem you're solving)
- Publish a "behind the scenes" or "making of" post to build narrative
- Activate early supporters: brief them on launch date, give them share templates
- Finalize all landing page copy with real testimonials and social proof

### Week 1: Final Prep
- Send second pre-launch email (reveal what's launching, build excitement)
- Test all links, forms, checkout flows, and tracking end-to-end
- Brief support team on new feature/product FAQs
- Prepare real-time engagement plan for launch day (who monitors what, response templates)
- Queue all scheduled posts — double-check times and links
- Do a dry run: click through the entire user journey from ad/post → landing → signup → onboard

---

## Phase 4: Launch Week

### Launch Day (Day 1)
- **Morning:** Publish blog post, activate landing page, send announcement email
- **Mid-morning:** Post across all social channels, submit to Product Hunt/directories
- **All day:** Monitor and respond to every comment, question, and mention in real-time
- **Evening:** Share early traction numbers with supporters, thank early adopters
- **Track:** Signups, page views, conversion rate, email opens, social engagement

### Day 2-3: Sustain Momentum
- Share user reactions, testimonials, and early wins on social
- Send follow-up email to non-openers with different subject line
- Engage in communities where your audience lives (Reddit, Slack groups, Discord)
- Publish a "launch lessons" or "day 1 results" thread (transparency builds trust)

### Day 4-5: Convert Stragglers
- Send "in case you missed it" email to remaining list
- Run retargeting ads to landing page visitors who didn't convert
- Post case study or detailed use case content
- Reach out personally to high-value prospects who showed interest

### Day 6-7: Transition to Growth
- Compile launch metrics: total signups, conversion rates, CAC, channel performance
- Identify top-performing channels and double down
- Collect structured feedback from new users (what almost stopped you from signing up?)
- Begin transitioning from "launch" messaging to "ongoing" messaging

---

## Phase 5: Post-Launch (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1 Post-Launch: Optimize & Learn
- Review all channel metrics — kill underperforming, scale what works
- Run post-signup survey: "How did you hear about us?" and "What almost stopped you?"
- Fix any UX friction points flagged during launch
- Publish roundup: "What we learned launching X" (content that compounds)

### Week 2-3 Post-Launch: Deepen Engagement
- Launch onboarding email sequence for new signups
- Publish comparison content: "X vs Y" pages targeting competitor keywords
- Create customer case study from best early adopter
- Start building content flywheel: repurpose launch content into blog posts, threads, short videos
- Set up referral program if product has network effects

### Week 4 Post-Launch: Sustain & Compound
- Transition from launch mode to content marketing mode
- Establish regular publishing cadence (1-2x/week blog, daily social)
- Build evergreen content around launch keywords
- Plan next launch moment (feature update, milestone, partnership)

---

## Phase 6: Long-Term Growth (Month 2-3)

### Compound the Launch
- Repurpose launch content: blog → newsletter → social threads → video
- Build SEO content cluster around your primary use case
- Develop referral loop: in-product sharing, affiliate program, word-of-mouth triggers
- Create "second launch" opportunities: major feature, pricing change, milestone (1K users)

### Metrics to Track Monthly
| Metric | What It Tells You | Action If Low |
|---|---|---|
| Signup rate | Landing page effectiveness | Revise copy, test new angles |
| Activation rate | Onboarding quality | Simplify first-run experience |
| Day 7 retention | Product-market fit signal | Survey churned users, fix gaps |
| Referral rate | Organic growth potential | Add sharing prompts, incentivize |
| CAC by channel | Channel efficiency | Reallocate budget to winners |

### Re-Launch Triggers
Not every product nails it the first time. Re-launch when:
- You've significantly improved the product based on feedback
- You've found a new positioning angle that resonates better
- You've identified a new audience segment
- You hit a milestone worth celebrating (100 customers, $10K MRR)
- A competitor stumbles and creates an opening

---

## Timeline Compression Guide

| Scenario | Compress To | What to Cut |
|---|---|---|
| Solo founder, simple product | 3-4 weeks total | Skip borrowed channels, minimize assets |
| Feature launch (existing product) | 2 weeks | Skip Phase 1, use existing channels |
| Urgent competitive response | 1 week | Blog + email + social only, skip PR |
| Funded team, major product | Keep 8 weeks | Don't compress — coordinate properly |

The timeline is a framework, not a cage. Ship when ready, not when the calendar says so.
