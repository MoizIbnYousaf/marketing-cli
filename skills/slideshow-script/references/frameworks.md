# Slideshow Scripting Frameworks

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Structure:**
1. **Attention** (Slide 1): Bold claim, shocking stat, or provocative question
2. **Interest** (Slides 2-3): Explain the mechanism, share the "how"
3. **Desire** (Slides 4-5): Show the outcome, paint the picture of "after"
4. **Action** (Slides 6-7): Clear CTA with URL/handle

**Best for:** Broad awareness, product introductions, feature showcases
**Visual pairing:** Typographic — progressive text revelation matches the framework's build-up

**Example pattern:**
- "30 seconds." → "That's how long 5 deep breaths take." → "Your phone opens when your breath is done." → "lumi.app"

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

**Structure:**
1. **Problem** (Slide 1-2): Name the pain the audience knows
2. **Agitate** (Slides 3-4): Make the pain worse, show consequences
3. **Solution** (Slides 5-7): Introduce the fix, prove it works, CTA

**Best for:** Pain-driven products, alternatives to existing solutions, "there's a better way"
**Visual pairing:** Contrast Play or Split — tension between problem and solution halves

**Example pattern:**
- "Every app blocker just makes you wait." → "Wait. Or breathe. Or read quotes. None of it sticks." → "Lumi makes you take a real pause. Then your phone opens."

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Structure:**
1. **Before** (Slides 1-2): Current reality (relatable struggle)
2. **After** (Slides 3-4): Dream state (what life looks like solved)
3. **Bridge** (Slides 5-7): How to get there (the product), CTA

**Best for:** Transformation stories, lifestyle products, habit change
**Visual pairing:** Atmospheric — emotional transformation needs breathing room

**Example pattern:**
- "My phone was the first thing I touched every morning." → "Now my breath is." → "Lumi is the bridge."

## Star-Story-Solution

**Structure:**
1. **Star** (Slide 1): Introduce a relatable character or persona
2. **Story** (Slides 2-5): Take them through a conflict or journey
3. **Solution** (Slides 6-7): The product resolves the story, CTA

**Best for:** Emotional connection, community-building, personal narratives
**Visual pairing:** Editorial — story flow needs magazine-like pacing with asymmetric text

**Example pattern:**
- "Maya unlocks her phone 150 times a day." → "One day she set Lumi. Five deep breaths before each unlock." → "Now her first act is presence."

## Stat-Flip

**Structure:**
1. **Stat** (Slide 1-2): Surprising, specific data point
2. **Flip** (Slides 3-4): Reframe the stat, challenge assumptions
3. **CTA** (Slides 5-6): Clear call to action anchored in the data

**Best for:** Data-driven audiences, proof points, credibility building
**Visual pairing:** Data-Led — giant numbers as visual anchors, text supports the data

**Example pattern:**
- "You unlock your phone 150 times a day." → "That's 150 chances to take a breath." → "Lumi turns screen time into breath time."

## Slide Type Reference

Each slide should be annotated with one of these types:

| Type | Visual Treatment | Animation |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| `stat` | Giant number (300px+), small label | Spring bouncy, counter tick |
| `anchor_word` | One massive word fills slide | Spring heavy/snappy |
| `emotional_pivot` | Centered text, generous whitespace | Smooth, glow pulse |
| `cascade` | Sequential lines, left-aligned | Smooth, staggered reveal |
| `cta` | Centered, ornamental, URL prominent | Smooth, sequential fade |
| `logo_intro` | Logo centered, clean backdrop | Spring bouncy, sparkle |
| `comparison` | Split layout, two zones | Slide transition |
| `question` | Large text with ? or ... | Smooth fade |
